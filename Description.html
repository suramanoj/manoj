University Courses and Descriptions

Computer Science 101
Introduction to computer science and programming. Covers basic algorithms, data structures, and the principles of software engineering.
Mathematics 201
Calculus II: Integration techniques, applications of the definite integral, sequences and series, and parametric equations and polar coordinates.
Physics 301
Classical Mechanics: Study of motion, forces, energy, momentum, and the laws governing physical systems. Includes laboratory experiments.
Biology 101
General Biology: Introduction to the principles of biology, including cell structure and function, genetics, evolution, and ecology.
Chemistry 202
Organic Chemistry: Focus on the structure, properties, and reactions of organic compounds. Includes laboratory work on synthesis and analysis of organic substances.
History 101
World History: Overview of major historical events and civilizations from ancient times to the modern era. Emphasis on cultural, social, and political developments.
Psychology 101
Introduction to Psychology: Study of human behavior and mental processes, including topics such as perception, cognition, emotion, and psychological disorders.
Economics 101
Principles of Microeconomics: Examination of individual markets, consumer behavior, production and costs, and the role of government in the economy.
Literature 201
American Literature: Study of major works and authors in American literature, with an emphasis on themes, literary techniques, and historical context.
Philosophy 101
Introduction to Philosophy: Exploration of fundamental philosophical questions and theories concerning knowledge, reality, and values.
